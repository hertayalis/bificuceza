Beth McLeod is a name that many people in northeast Ohio recognize. She was a TV meteorologist for more than a decade, delivering the forecast with accuracy and enthusiasm. But in 2019, her life took a tragic turn when she lost her husband and her mother in a short span of time. She decided to make a radical career change and became a nightclub owner, creating some of the most unique and fun nightlife destinations in Cleveland. In this article, we will explore how Beth McLeod went from weather to nightlife, and how she found a new purpose and joy in her life.

Losing her Best Friend

Beth McLeod was married to Fred McLeod for over 28 years. A reunion that has seen the pair climb through the ranks of journalism. While Fred worked as a play-by-play sportscaster for the Cleveland Cavaliers, Beth made a name for herself as a meteorologist. 

In September 2019, Fred McLeod passed away suddenly after suffering from a massive heart attack. The death of Fred came as a shock to the fans but the one in the most pain was Beth. After all, the couple had been together for 28 years and always made sure that they didn’t stay apart for more than three days.

“For a good three days my life was just a blur of a good two weeks of time. I don’t remember the funeral. I don’t remember the visitation. I don’t remember a lot. It’s like you’ve been hit by a bus and you can’t get up. It’s really a heavy feeling and I can’t describe it and some days are better than others.”

Things went from bad to worse for Beth, who lost her mother after five weeks of losing her husband. Not taking anything away from the grief, Beth had already been mourning her mother for two years.

“It’s hard to tell the story because I don’t think many people believe that, like, my husband died suddenly and then my mom died but I think my mom had been sick for two years. So, not that that’s easier, but I think I’ve been mourning her for two years as she progressed.”

Rising Through the Ranks

Beth began her professional career at CBS Sports in New York but later moved to WDIV in Detroit, Michigan. She stayed in Michigan for over 17 years. She left WDIV to be closer to Fred, who at the time was working in Cleveland. She joined  WOIO in Cleveland and started working as a weather forecaster and worked for almost 10 years before joining Fox 8 News Channel in Cleveland as a resident meteorologist. 

Switching Career- Beth Became a Nightclub Owner in 2020

Beth McLeod is a former meteorologist who switched to becoming a nightclub owner after the death of her husband, Fred McLeod, in 2019. She bought the disco Good Night John Boy in 2020, during the peak of the pandemic, and joined Forward Hospitality Group, a company that owns, operates and creates new nightlife and restaurant concepts. She said that buying the nightclub not only changed her life but saved it. She also opened two more bars, Welcome to the Farm and XMAS Bar, in 2021 and 2022 respectively. Her boyfriend, Bobby Sotka, is in charge of operating the clubs. She said that she enjoys creating memories, friendships, and happy times for her customers.
